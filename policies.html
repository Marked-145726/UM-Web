Student Conduct

The UM Student Code of Conduct defines expected behavior of all enrolled students on and off campus. Students must uphold the dignity of the university, respect the rights of fellow community members, and comply with all university rules, Philippine laws, and applicable regulations.

The Student Discipline Office processes violations. Students are afforded due process rights, including the right to be heard, to present evidence, and to appeal decisions.

In compliance with the Anti-Sexual Harassment Act (Republic Act 7877) and Safe Spaces Act (R.A. 11313), UM prohibits all forms of sexual harassment in the university environment. A Committee on Decorum and Investigation (CODI) is established on each campus to receive and adjudicate complaints.

All confirmed violations will result in administrative sanctions. The university ensures confidentiality and protection from retaliation for all complainants.

Data Privacy

In compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act 10173), the University of Mindanao is committed to protecting the personal information of its students, faculty, staff, and stakeholders. A Data Protection Officer (DPO) is designated on each campus.

Personal data is collected and processed only for legitimate institutional purposes. Data subjects have the right to access, correct, and object to the processing of their personal information. Data breaches are reported to the National Privacy Commission within 72 hours of discovery.

Financial Affairs

Tuition and fees are set by the Board of Trustees in accordance with CHED regulations and are published before the start of enrollment each academic year. Full disclosure of all fees is required before students complete enrollment.

Refunds follow a pro-rated schedule: 100% refund before the start of classes; 80% in Week 1; 60% in Week 2; 40% in Week 3; no refund after Week 4 except for medical, family emergency, or CHED-mandated reasons with proper documentation.

UM administers institutional scholarships (Academic Excellence, Athletic, Cultural, Need-Based), government scholarships (CHED, UNIFAST, PESFA, GAD), and private donor scholarships. Scholarship applications are accepted each enrollment period and evaluated by the Scholarship Office.

Recipients must maintain the required GPA, comply with scholarship conditions, and report any change in financial or academic status.
